Ram Rahman

Photographer, Curator, and Activist

Ram Rahman (born 1955) is one of India's most prominent contemporary photographers and curators, based in Delhi. Armed with degrees in Physics from MIT and Graphic Design from the Yale University School of Art, his multifaceted career bridges the gap between structured documentation and profound artistic expression.

Known for his archival impulse, Rahman's photography captures the social fabric of an evolving, post-Nehruvian India. His lens often focuses on architectural modernism—documenting the structures designed by his father, Habib Rahman—alongside vibrant street photography and poignant portraits of artists and intellectuals.

As a founding member of SAHMAT (the Safdar Hashmi Memorial Trust), Rahman has dedicated decades to resisting communal forces through cultural action, famously curating the award-winning "The Sahmat Collective: Art and Activism in India since 1989" exhibit.
